STEP 1 OF 7

Clinical Context & Problem Definition

Before we look at any data, we define the clinical problem. In Cardiology, we want to predict which patients are most likely to be readmitted to hospital within 30 days after a heart failure discharge — so doctors can arrange follow-up care in advance.

⏱ ~3 minutes
Clinical Scenario
Cardiology
Will this patient be readmitted to hospital within 30 days of discharge following a heart failure episode?
30% of heart failure patients are readmitted within 30 days. Each readmission costs approximately €15,000. Early identification allows nurses to arrange discharge follow-up calls and medication checks.
What ML cannot do: It cannot replace your clinical judgment. It can flag high-risk patients, but you make the final decision.

Real-World AI Failures in Healthcare — What Goes Wrong
⚠ Case 1: Sepsis Algorithm Bias
An ICU sepsis prediction algorithm was found to perform significantly worse for Black patients because it was trained mostly on data from white patients. Hospitals had to suspend the tool and retrain it with representative data.
⚠ Case 2: Accuracy ≠ Safety
A readmission model with 85% overall accuracy was deployed but had only 30% sensitivity for elderly patients — the highest-risk group. The hospital reported more missed readmissions after deploying the AI than before.
How to Prevent This
Always check subgroup performance. Always require human review. Always retrain periodically. Never deploy based on overall accuracy alone.
